We were delighted to host [Greg Chojnowski](https://gchojnowski.github.io/) at
Newcastle for a one-day EM model building tutorial and lecture, taking
participants through a complete cycle of model building into cryo-EM maps.

The whole course ran in the browser. No local installs, no specialist hardware —
just a pool of cloud virtual servers, which meant everyone could get straight to
the science rather than wrestling with software setup. Two tools did the heavy
lifting: **Moorhen** for interactive model building, which proved fast and stable
enough for even the more demanding tasks, and **CCP-EM/Doppio** to guide people
through the model building pipeline.

![Participants working through the tutorial]({{ '/assets/images/workshop-2.jpg' | relative_url }})

A one-day crash course is intense, and it's easy to underestimate how many
skills quietly go into interactive model building. Huge thanks to Greg for
running such a well-judged session, and to everyone who took part — a great
group, with great questions throughout.
